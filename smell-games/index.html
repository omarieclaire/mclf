Smell Games

A series of games I made to play with your nose!

I smell you

(4-12 players, one blindfold)

One player ('the smeller') smells the wrist of each other player, one at a time. The smeller is then blindfolded and smells each player again in random order. The smeller tries to guess who they are smelling. If the smeller misidentifies a player, that player becomes the new smeller and the game begins again.

What’s that smell?

(2-50 players, one blindfold)

All players except one close their eyes. The one remaining player (the gatherer) finds or concocts a smell (for example: grass, cinnamon, coffee, or soap) and offers it to each player for smelling. After each player has had a chance to smell the item, the gatherer counts to three and all players shout what they think they smelled. Then a different player becomes the gatherer.

Solo smeller

(1-50 players, closed eyes)

A player walks around a space stopping at objects, closing their eyes, and smelling each object deeply, for at least three deep inhales. Player pays special attention to feelings and thoughts associated with the smells.

pea-YOU

(3-50 players, one blindfold)

A game where players compete to find the worst smelling item. One player is the designated smeller, and must be blindfolded. All players are divided into two teams. Every turn, each team tries to find or concoct the worst smell. At the end of each turn, the smeller points at the winning team and says “pea-YOU!” At the end of 5 rounds, the losing team must offer up a new person to be the smeller.

Chose your own smell-adventure

(1-10 players, 1-10 identical zip-lock bags or jars)

Each player collects a smell in identical jars or bags. All players sit in a circle put their jars in the centre of the circle, intentionally mixing them up. Each player chooses a jar and closes their eyes. Then the players take turns telling a story, each using one sentence and integrating the smell in their jar into the story.

Can you smell it?

(2-50 players, no equipment needed)

Players are divided into two teams. One player vividly describes a recognizable place or experience only by smells while the opposing team tries to guess what they are describing.
