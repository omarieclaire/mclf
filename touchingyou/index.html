Touching You:
An Exploration of Touch

Touching You is an interactive audio/video installation evoking the experience of touch through the imagination. Touching You invites you to deeply re-inhabit your body and reconnect with the idea of touch through a series of ritual touch statements, using allegory and sensorial imagination.

A collaboration between a video-game designer, contemporary dancers, electronic musicians, and the public, Touching You is a curious exploration of touch as a fundamental joining together of people, of reminding us that we have been in relation all along.

These days we feel a deep aversion to touch. We are fearful of handshakes, sidling away from strangers on the street. We are isolated, lonely scrolling social media feeds, fingers running over smooth plastic and cool metal. Our fingertips dream of brushing, caressing, feeling, scratching, stroking, and the soft pressure of intentional touch on our skin.

We touch only through computers, tapping on keys, sliding on trackpads, clicking on mice. We are chemically aroused by tiny notification bubbles, endless shitposts, incessant chatter. We try to reach through our computers to touch people in Zoom calls, touching them with our eyes and being touched by the vibrations in our ears as their voices filter through tinny speakers.

Meanwhile, the constant, non-consensual touch of surveillance technology, watching our tentative movements, an enormous hand on the small of our backs.

Touch is a fundamental joining together, a bringing of two things in relation, assessing the relation of things. Touch is an opening to sensation.

Eryn: We are always self-contained, connection is an imaginary thing that happens between our bodies.

Marie: We are always already connected, aloneness in an imaginary thing.

The reception of touch is more than a translation, it opens another dimension to transform and complexify the intention. Neither intent or reception are extricable from context, from the space of the whole.
